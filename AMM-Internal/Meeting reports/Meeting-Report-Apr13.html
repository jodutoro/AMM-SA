Manick Bhan · Research Oracle
Research Oracle — passive blind spot detection (HTML setup file)
Manick's personal intelligence system. An insight agent reads personal data (voice notes, Claude Code session logs, team chats) from an Obsidian Vault, identifies blind spots using analytical lenses, then a research agent picks the top 3 topics and queries Gemini Deep Research for live data — all passively, on a scheduled cron. A Claude Code SessionEnd hook continuously feeds session context into the vault. Setup: drop the provided HTML file into Claude Code and say "implement this." Requires Obsidian + Gemini CLI. Real example: the Oracle recommended standardizing the email layer across SA's 80 microservices — a solution the team hadn't considered. Added to KB.
Justin Rondeau · Google Ads Audit

A playbook that analyzes historical ad performance across accounts and recommends budget reallocation from underperforming keywords to high-intent keywords. Strategic frameworks and consensus views that emerged from the group discussion.

Land & Expand
Month one is always the least they'll ever pay you
Manick's enterprise model: land with the service the client came for, prove ROI using agentic tools, then expand into the full stack — PPC, content, technical SEO, CRO, social. LinkGraph data: every cohort has expanded spend over time, none have churned. The expansion rate over the last 6 months is the highest in company history. Key: show them a unified analytics dashboard so they see the ROI clearly — that's what builds the conviction to keep spending.
Positioning · Arman Advani
"Strategy by humans, execution by AI, QA by experts"
The consensus positioning framework for agencies in 2026: don't hide AI usage, lean into it. The Tesla analogy — AI doesn't go anywhere until a human tells it where to go, and a human is always ready to take the wheel. Your competitive advantage is not that you use AI, it's that you use it the right way. Clients who don't hear this narrative will find agencies that tell it better. Budget stretches further, timelines compress, more gets done with less.
Pricing · Group Discussion
Bolt-on marketing department: $3K–$5K/mo controls all 4 channels
The emerging model: position as a bolt-on marketing department controlling the "core four" — search, advertising, website, CRM/automation. Clayton Joyner: "The more hooks you have in someone, the higher your retention is. The more valuable you are, the more pain there is to change." Current average: $3K–$5K/client. At 150 vertically-specialized clients: 2 account managers + everything else runs in Search Atlas. 65% gross margin achievable when controlling all 4 elements. Low-ticket ($297/mo) debated but consensus is it creates too much service liability at thin margins.

SA MCP → Website Studio → Claude Code → CMS MCP (the 4-step workflow)

Specific insights, techniques, and direct quotes worth carrying forward.

Manick Bhan on Content Quality
Homepage depth 1–2 pages determine your entire quality score
The secret to reviving dormant sites and ranking new domains: make the pages that are directly linked from the homepage exceptional. Google evaluates the site based on the homepage and everything one node in — that's how it calculates quality. If those pages are outstanding and you add a few backlinks, rankings follow fast. "It doesn't even matter what the history is. If you prune out the garbage content and rebuild, these sites can come back to life."
Manick Bhan on Research Oracle
Passive vs. active intelligence — the industry is moving to passive
"Right now we still live with a question — you ask the question and the agent gives you the answer. Where we're going is passive. I've already asked a bunch of questions today. I don't need to ask another question. You just analyze the questions I've already asked and come up with new information that's relevant that I didn't think about." Use a separate Obsidian vault from your client vault — you want the Oracle focused on you, not over-indexed on client content. Use Gemini CLI (not Claude) for the deep research — Google search is necessary for live data.
Clayton Joyner on Credits & COGS
Build a credit estimator to productize your service packages
Clayton's insight: to scale with any vertical, you need to know the credit cost of each playbook so you can model COGS. His ask: a "Domino's pizza tracker" for website builds — every stage visible to the client and internally. Upcoming SA feature: full quota history with timestamps and per-user logs. You can set credit caps per client in agent rules. Clayton and Bryan are both working toward structured playbooks that turn into repeatable packages with known cost profiles.
Manick Bhan on Site Networks
Satellite sites — your own network of digital experiments
Build your own network of satellite sites. Run experiments on them. Use them to reinforce ranking on your primary sites. Use them for affiliate plays. Use them for paid media landing pages. Use them for comparison content Google won't allow on your brand site. CloudStacks are adding 20+ new sites (currently 14, going to 35+). Every indexed CloudStack link is additive — especially when they interlink. Manick's team is running CloudStacks to every landing page, blog post, and service page for all enterprise clients.
Arman Advani on Content Strategy
Start with SA keyword + entity data before building — not after
Most agencies do it backwards — build the site, then try to optimize the content. The right order: run the SA keyword research + competitor analysis + local market data first. Figure out which keywords have realistic ranking shots before writing a single word. The Domain Knowledge Network (DKN) tool in Auto recommends the full sitemap structure with keyword targets per page. Feed this into Content Genius which automatically scrapes SERP competitors, analyzes entity usage, and writes with those patterns embedded — no external SEO research step needed.
